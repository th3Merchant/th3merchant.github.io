The real problem

Frequent carrying can wear out your arms, shoulders, and back.

Babies often want to be held during errands, walks, family visits, and daily routines. Holding them on one hip without support can feel fine for a few minutes, then tiring quickly.

The structured hip seat gives your child a more stable place to sit while helping shift the load away from your arms and shoulders.

Simple setup

No wrapping, no complicated carrier routine.

This is not a full baby wrap. It is a structured hip seat with an adjustable waist strap, made for moments when you need quick support without managing long fabric, padding, or ties.

  • Firm no-foam seat design
  • Adjustable waist strap
  • Quick on-and-off support
  • Useful for short errands and frequent carrying
Multiple positions

Useful as your child grows and changes positions.

Tushbaby supports face-to-face, forward-facing, and side-carry positions. That flexibility matters because babies and toddlers do not always want to be carried the same way.

It can be especially helpful during those quick transitions when a child wants up, down, then up again.

Built for errands

Storage pockets make quick trips easier.

One of the strongest practical features is the built-in storage. Five pockets and a tuck-away bottle holder help keep essentials close when you do not want to carry a full diaper bag for every short outing.

  • Five storage pockets
  • Tuck-away bottle holder
  • Space for small parent essentials
  • Useful for malls, walks, airports, and errands
